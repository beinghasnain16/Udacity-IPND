Jaguar

The jaguar is the 3rd largest of the big cats after the tiger and the lion and it is the largest of all the big cats in the Americas.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera onca
  • Average Length: 120 to 200 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 12 to 15 years
  • Habitat: Forests

Jaguars are large cats that can be found in North, Central and South America. They are identified by their yellow or orange coats, dark spots and short legs. Jaguar has the strongest jaw structure of any feline. Although jaguars look very much like leopards and are closely related to them, their ecological role and behaviour is more similar to that of the tiger.
